## White light is a crowd of colors

Sunlight may look white, but it carries the full visible rainbow. Each color travels as a wave with a different wavelength. Red light has a relatively long wavelength, while blue and violet light have shorter ones.

When sunlight enters Earth's atmosphere, it runs into tiny molecules of nitrogen and oxygen. Those molecules are much smaller than the wavelengths of visible light.

## Tiny molecules favor tiny waves

The molecules scatter shorter wavelengths much more strongly than longer wavelengths. Blue light gets knocked away from the Sun's direct beam and sent bouncing through the atmosphere in every direction.

That means when you look at almost any patch of clear sky, some of the blue light scattered by the air is traveling toward your eyes.

## Sunsets tell the same story

Near sunset, sunlight takes a much longer route through the atmosphere. So much blue light scatters out of the direct beam that the light still traveling toward you is richer in oranges and reds.

The blue noon sky and the red sunset are two views of the same process.
