Astronauts do not float because space forgot about gravity. They float because gravity is pulling on absolutely everything around them at once.

Astronauts and their spacecraft are falling toward Earth together. The spacecraft is also moving sideways so quickly that Earth curves away beneath it, turning the fall into an orbit. Nothing inside needs a floor to hold it up.

The orbiting spacecraft keeps missing Earth as it falls. Its forward speed carries it beyond the curved horizon at about the same rate gravity pulls it downward, so the fall can continue around the planet. Orbit is falling with enough sideways speed.

Gravity is still strong aboard the International Space Station. Astronauts feel weightless because everything around them shares the same fall, not because gravity has disappeared.

Orbit is not a place where falling stops. It is a fall so fast and sideways that it keeps missing the ground.

Inside the spacecraft, a dropped pencil, a sleeping astronaut, and the floor itself are all sharing the same endless fall.
