Stand outside and Earth feels perfectly still while the entire sky appears to parade around it. An Earth-centered universe was not a ridiculous first guess.

Earth feels still while the Sun, Moon, planets, and stars appear to move overhead. That everyday view made an Earth-centered universe a reasonable early model, and astronomers built sophisticated calculations around it.

The hardest clue was planetary retrograde motion, when a planet seems to reverse direction for a while. Earth-centered models explained it with loops upon loops; a Sun-centered model made the apparent reversal a result of moving planets passing one another.

Old geocentric models were not foolish guesses; some predicted planetary positions fairly well. Replacing them required better observations plus a new explanation of how a moving Earth could feel stationary.

Changing the model required people to trust careful observations over the powerful feeling that the ground beneath them could not possibly be moving.
