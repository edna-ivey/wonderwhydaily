Dreams can hand you a talking staircase, an old friend, and a flying school bus, then make the whole arrangement feel perfectly reasonable.

Dreams emerge while the sleeping brain stays active, mixing memory fragments, emotions, predictions, and spontaneous signals into experiences. Scientists are still testing their purposes, and no single explanation fits every dream.

A sleeping brain is not simply replaying a recording. It can join a recent conversation, an old place, and a made-up person into one scene, creating a story that never existed while you were awake. Memory supplies pieces, not a finished script.

During vivid REM dreams, visual and emotional brain areas can be busy while some regions used for careful reasoning are less active. That helps explain why bizarre dream events can feel completely normal.

A dream is not a video stored in your head. It is the sleeping brain remixing pieces into an experience that has never existed before.
