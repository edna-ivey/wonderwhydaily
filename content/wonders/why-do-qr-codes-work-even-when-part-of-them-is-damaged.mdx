A QR code can lose a corner under a scratch, sticker, or splash and still open the right page. It is not guessing. It is carrying spare clues.

The black-and-white grid stores a message as blocks of data. Alongside that message sits error-correction information created with mathematics. When a scanner finds a missing or incorrect block, those extra relationships can help reconstruct what should have been there.

Imagine tearing a few words from a sentence that also includes carefully designed clues about every line. You might rebuild the gaps without seeing the original. A QR code performs a much more precise version of that trick with numbers.

The large squares near its corners help a scanner locate and orient the code. Smaller timing patterns help organize the grid. Damage those important guides, or cover too much of the message, and the scan will eventually fail. The code is resilient, not invincible.

Designers can choose stronger error correction for codes likely to face dirt or wear, although the protection leaves less room for the main message. Every scratched QR code that still works is a tiny public performance of mathematical preparation.
