## Near things slide, far things stay

Imagine looking through a car window. A fence beside the road appears to race backward. A distant hill moves much more slowly. The Moon is farther away than either of them, by an almost unimaginable amount.

As you move, your viewing angle to nearby objects changes quickly. Your viewing angle to the Moon changes by such a tiny amount that you cannot notice it during an ordinary trip.

## Your brain makes a reasonable guess

Because the Moon stays in nearly the same place in your field of view while everything nearby moves, your brain interprets it as keeping pace with you.

It is not actually following your car. You and the Moon are simply too far apart for a short drive to create much visible shift.

## Distance changes perspective

The effect is a lovely reminder that motion is always relative to something else. Nearby scenery gives you one sense of movement; the distant Moon gives you another.
