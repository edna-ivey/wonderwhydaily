## First, find the ground

A cat's inner ear helps it sense which way is up. Once it detects that it is falling, it bends in the middle and turns the front half of its body toward the ground.

The cat tucks its front legs and extends its back legs. That makes the front half easier to rotate and the rear half harder to rotate.

## Then, switch the shape

Next the cat changes its pose: it extends the front legs and tucks the back legs. Now the rear half can turn more easily until it lines up with the front.

These opposite twists let the whole cat reorient without needing to push on the air or rely entirely on its tail.

## Flexible does not mean invincible

Cats also spread their legs and arch their backs to help manage a landing. But the righting reflex does not make falls safe. Cats can still be seriously injured, especially when they do not have enough time or space to turn.
