Say an interesting word to a dog and you may get one of nature's most charming question marks: the head tilt.

Tilting changes how a sound reaches each ear and may help a dog inspect where it came from. It can also change the dog's view of your face, especially for breeds whose muzzles block part of what they see.

There may not be one reason behind every tilt. A dog could be locating a sound, trying to see you better, responding to a familiar phrase, or combining all three in one very photogenic moment.

Dogs learn from our reactions too. If a head tilt earns smiles, attention, or another interesting word, the dog may be more likely to offer that behavior again.

That adorable tilt may be several useful experiments happening at once: listen better, see better, and check whether the human is about to say something important.

There may be no single translation for the gesture, which makes each tilt feel less like a programmed trick and more like active investigation.
