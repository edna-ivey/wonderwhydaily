Mirrors have been blamed for swapping left and right for centuries. The strange truth is that they never agreed to do that job.

A mirror flips the direction pointing toward and away from its surface. We interpret that depth reversal as left-right because we imagine turning around to stand where our reflection appears to be.

Raise your right hand and the reflection raises the hand directly opposite it, which is still on your right side of the mirror. The confusion begins when you imagine the reflection as another person facing you.

If you lie sideways, a mirror still does not begin swapping up and down. It keeps doing the same thing it always did: reflecting front-to-back depth while your brain chooses how to compare the image.

A mirror is perfectly consistent; the confusing part is the imaginary turn your brain performs when it compares you with the person in the glass.
