# Milestone 1.7 Brand Refinement Review

**Status:** Concepts and recommendations only. No live application changes.

## Logo Concepts

The visual comparison board is available at
[`milestone-1.7-concepts.svg`](./milestone-1.7-concepts.svg).

All three concepts use the existing forest green, lime, cream, circular fields,
orbital paths, dots, and editorial wordmark. In every concept, `DAILY` is
centered beneath `Wonder Why`.

### 1. The Curiosity Trail — Recommended

An open, imperfect path circles through the mark and ends at a lime dot. It
suggests following a question without using a literal question mark. The open
path keeps it from reading as an atom or planet.

### 2. Many Worlds

Two non-crossing orbital paths connect dots and a small sparkle. This represents
the site's broad range of subjects and feels closest to the existing category
art. It is more detailed, so it may lose clarity at favicon size.

### 3. The Wonder Portal

A dark center inside a lime circle creates a doorway or window into discovery.
It has the strongest editorial presence, but its heavier shape would become a
more noticeable brand shift.

## Question-Mark Alternatives

### Option A: Remove It

The cleanest and most timeless treatment. It lets each category symbol remain
the only focal point, but removes some of the illustration's playful movement.

### Option B: Orbiting Dot — Recommended

A small forest-and-lime dot sits directly on the orbit path. It feels native to
the existing illustration system and adds movement without competing with the
category symbol.

### Option C: Sparkle

A small cream sparkle adds delight and works especially well for Weird &
Wonderful. Used on every category, however, it may feel more decorative than
editorial.

## Homepage Copy Recommendations

The current hero and mission section repeat the daily-question promise in close
proximity. Keep the homepage structure unchanged and replace only the hero
value-proposition line after approval.

### Recommended

**Make room for wonder.**

Why it works: short, warm, and broad enough for every category. The question
directly beneath it supplies the concrete invitation.

### Alternative 2

**There is more to notice.**

Why it works: thoughtful and editorial, with a quiet invitation to look again.

### Alternative 3

**Follow your curiosity.**

Why it works: clear and active, though it overlaps with the existing category
section kicker.

Recommendation: keep the mission copy in the intro section as the one explicit
explanation of the product. Remove the duplicate promise from the hero rather
than rewriting approved mission copy.

## About Page Mockup Approach

Keep every word and use the existing wide and reading containers.

1. Open with a dark forest editorial masthead using `Wonder Why Daily` and the
   first sentence as the deck.
2. Treat “The best questions often lead to the most surprising discoveries.”
   as a large pull quote with a lime rule.
3. Keep the remaining mission copy in a calm, narrow reading column with more
   deliberate paragraph grouping.
4. Give `Where It Started` its own cream-on-forest or parchment-toned story
   panel, using the birthday date as a small editorial marker.
5. End with one restrained `Stay Curious.` sign-off rather than making both
   instances compete visually.

This changes presentation only. It does not require rewriting or removing any
approved copy.

## Footer Refinement Approach

Split the footer into two visually distinct regions:

1. **Explore More Projects:** a quiet publication-family section above the
   footer, separated by whitespace and a thin rule. Project names become proper
   text links; descriptions remain secondary. Confirm external destinations
   before implementation. The current site links internally to `/`; a coming
   soon project should link to its real landing page when available.
2. **Founder line:** a compact dark-forest footer containing only the centered
   line: “Started as an 11th birthday gift. Built for curious minds. ✨”

The founder message becomes the final, memorable note. The projects remain
discoverable without feeling like advertising.

## Recommended Direction

- Logo: **The Curiosity Trail**
- Illustration detail: **Orbiting Dot**
- Homepage hero line: **Make room for wonder.**
- About: editorial masthead, pull quote, and distinct founder-story panel
- Footer: publication-family section followed by one centered founder line
